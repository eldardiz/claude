## Proposal Philosophy

Goal: start a conversation, not get hired immediately.

1. FOCUS ON THEM: Mirror their language. Diagnose their problem. Don't list credentials.
2. ALWAYS END WITH A QUESTION: Low-friction, easy to say yes to. Never close with a statement.
3. DIAGNOSE A GAP: One specific observation that proves you did homework no one else did.
4. LOOM ON HIGH-VALUE JOBS ($3k+): 60-90s Loom pulling up their site with a specific insight.
5. STRUCTURE FOR SKIMMERS: 150-250 words. Hook, case studies, timeline, question.
6. SPECIFICITY WINS: Named clients, live URLs, hard numbers beat vague claims every time.

## Writing Rules

- No em dashes (use commas or periods)
- No AI phrasing ("I'd be happy to", "leverage", "I'm confident I can deliver")
- No generic openers ("Thanks for posting this job")
- 150-250 words
- Hook mirrors their language
- Named clients, real URLs, real numbers
- Always include portfolio link
- ALWAYS end with a question

## Client Message Templates

### Discovery call
```
Hi [Name], I've looked over the brief. Before I send a detailed quote, would you be open
to a quick 15-minute call? I want to confirm requirements and make sure we're aligned before
scoping anything. I'm available [options]. Does any of those work?
```

### Scope change
```
Hi [Name], happy to add [request]. Quick note: this is outside our original scope covering
[original scope] and will need roughly [X hours] of extra work.

Options: (1) add to current contract (+$X), (2) scope as follow-up after delivery,
(3) swap with [lower-priority item]. What works best?
```

### Review request
```
Hi [Name], really enjoyed working on [project]. Seeing [specific result] is exactly why
I do this. If you have 2 minutes, a review on Upwork would mean a lot — link is in your
contracts page. Mention Webflow and B2B if you can. Reach out anytime.
Eldar
```

### Vague/changing requirements
```
Hi [Name], want to make sure I understand before I proceed. As I read it, you'd like me
to [restate in your words] — is that right? Once confirmed I can have it done within [X].
```

## Rate & Negotiation

- Lowball: "My rate reflects [experience/results]. I can't go lower without cutting quality — but I could suggest a smaller initial phase if budget is fixed."
- Rate raise: "Starting [date], my rate moves to $[X]/hr. I'd love to keep working together — let me know how you'd like to proceed."

## Red Flags

- "Pay after go-live" / no milestone payments
- "It's simple" + 50-line description
- No assets or access before scoping
- "Need it in 3 days" for a 3-month scope
- "Previous developer disappeared" — ask why
- "We'll figure out copy later" — copy-first or parallel, never last
- Wants full creative control but also your strategic expertise
